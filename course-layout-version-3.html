About the AP CSP Course


In Fall 2016, the College Board introduced the newest AP course AP Computer Science Principles (CSP), in order to further push the CS education. In the past 2016-2017 academic year, over 2700 schools offered the CSP course, and about 45000 participated in the first AP CSP exam.

AP CSP gives students the freedom to choose any programing language they want to use. In addition, it also enocurages students to use graphical languages such as Scratch, MIT App Inventor, and Snap etc. Compared with the traditional AP CS A, the new AP course highlights a wider range of CS topics, but less intensive on programming skills. Many schools consider CSP as a good prerequisite for AP CS A.

The goal of this course is to help students build the basic AP CSP knowledge base and the programming skills. We choose to teach students MIT App Inventor so that they can also build more advanced projects to enrich their technology experience for the college application.
